Duke CS Department Website:
Design

Type: Remote Internship

Timeframe: July - September 2020

Skills: Web Design, Teamwork

Tools: Adobe XD

Overview

Objective

Redesigning the Duke University Computer Science Department's website by creating prototypes of the main landing pages with a focus on user experience that is being implemented to their website.

My Role

Working on a team of 2 interns, 1 faculty project lead, and 2 computer science IT staff, I was responsible for the design and research aspect of our project. Throughout this internship, I...

  • Met with the team weekly to discuss the designs and the user experience
  • Engaged with the other intern in the human-centered design process by leading meetings
  • Created prototypes for all main landing pages in Adobe XD
  • Recieved feedback on the designs from Prof. Jun Yung, the CS Department Chair

Research

The current website experience

Here's the initial Duke CS Department's website. It gets the job done, but it can be tricky to find information and isn't very visually appealing.




Unsurprisingly, we found that visitors often struggle navigating through the website. The lack of engaging content created difficulties when finding the links for the most important information.

Ideation

How might we create a visually appealing website that enables visitors to easily navigate to what they need?

Task analysis

When my team began thinking about what pages had the most traffic on, we decided the homepage and the undergraduate, graduate, and research landing page were the ones we would focus on.

Website Restrictions
  • Adhere to the Duke Brand Guide
  • Needs to be compatible with the current Drupal framework being used to develop the website
  • The header and footer sections in Duke blue and the arrangement of the navigation in black
  • All colors and logo fonts come from the Duke palette, but the blues and green are most essential based on how we're identifying CS as part of the university and as a part of the Natural Sciences
  • The large featured photo is a common theme being used on Trinity websites. However, it would be useful to have more information conveyed, possibly by the use of other design elements
  • Keep the infographics that demonstrate "points of pride", although how this is displayed can be altered
Brainstorming pages

Homepage:

  • Convey more information than just a large picture
  • Utilize the empty space in the wide side gutters, or at least reduce their width
  • Improve the aesthetic appeal of the homepage by the use of a larger variety of colors in the Duke palette, tighter fonts, and more innovative design elements (unevenness, tiles, overlays, etc.)

Landing Pages:

  • Design principle of these pages should be aligned with that of the homepage
  • Less text and more graphics
  • Clear summary of how the section is organized and easy navigation
  • A richer palette and innovative design elements
  • Highlight the main messages instead of giving detailed information

Main Design Goals
  • An easy-to-navigate website experience
  • Aesthetically appealing designs
  • More information to be accessible with less scrolling
  • Provide concise information for readers to understand

Designing

Stepping into Design

With all the restrictions we already had before starting the designs, I began creating an idea of the color pallete, the website aesthetic, and what the homepage would look like.

Homepage

By using a grid-based layout, we started off with a simple design including all required sections: Events, Latest News, Research, and Contact.

From the first design to the second, we rearranged the pride points at the top of the page, changed the background colors of the sections, expanded the latest news section to be a gallery. The most difficult section was the Contact because I had a hard time figuring out how to organize the different social platforms.

Continuing the changes in design, we had incorporated more colors to create an inviting aesthetic. We also got rid of the Featured Video section and replaced it with an Our People because the Contact section would display the youtube videos.

In addition, I edited the footer section by adding the social media icons to the footer so all pages of the website would allow you to reach the different social media platforms and also simplified the quick links under each section of the footer.

We finally were finished with our homepage and ready to tackle the three main landing pages!

Landing Pages

We created a consistent aesthetic that we made sure to incorporate in the other landing pages. By using the same fonts, color pallete, and grid-based layout, we designed the Undergraduate, Graduate, and Research landing pages.








Feedback

Once we finished our designs, we were able to reach out to Professor Jun Yang, the CS Department Chair, to recieve feedback with our designs for the department's website. He suggested we include pride points on the landing pages for Undergraduate, Graduate and the Research section as well.

Improvements and Changes

Based on Professor Yang's Feedback, I made these changes to the website design using a similar design on the homepage of overlaying the pictures with text and badges.

We also decided as a team to make one final change on the Our people section from the homepage to reflect the Research section's design.

Takeaways

Knowing the audience

Think about our audience! When developing our designs we continuously asked ourselves How will the typical audience interact with our design?. With this in mind, I was able to focus on the user experience while incorporating the information needed.

Empathy for teammates

Teamwork makes the [UX] dream work! Because the internship was remote, we as a team understood the challenges that come with working from home. We scheduled meetings according to everyone's availability first and foremost.

Adhering to Restrictions

One of the difficulties when developing an academic institution's website is the requirement to adhere to specific guidelines. The team had to actively think about the image of Duke Trinity College of Arts & Science and make sure we do not design a website that would not be representative of the typical Trinity website.

Improved Design Skills

After completing this internship, I believe I have gained a valuable understanding of important design principles. The importance of simplicity, easy navigation, grid-based layouts, and consistency are some of the things I kept in mind when trying to create an aesthetic, user-friendly website design.
